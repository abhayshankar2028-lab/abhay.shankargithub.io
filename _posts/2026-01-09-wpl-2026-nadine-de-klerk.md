The highly anticipated Women's Premier League (WPL) 2026 has kicked off in Navi Mumbai, with the Mumbai Indians struggling to contain India International's (IISc) talented Nadine de Klerk. De Klerk's explosive batting display saw her score 108 not out, including four boundaries and two sixes, to help IISc post 183/5. 

Meanwhile, Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) successfully defended their opening-night lead in a thrilling match against the hapless Mumbai Indians. With 46 balls left, RCB captain, Mithali Raj, led her team to a dominant victory with a stunning 72-ball knock, culminating in a 7-ball boundary. RCB ultimately outscored IISc by 25 runs and secured their first win in the tournament. A gripping match that showcased the best of women's cricket is sure to leave fans eagerly anticipating the next thrilling encounter.
