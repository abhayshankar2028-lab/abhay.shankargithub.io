Captain Michael Bracewell has expressed his gratitude and immense pride after finally breaking through to reach 100 Test caps for the Black Caps. Bracewell, who captained the team from 2021 to 2023, revealed that his family's unwavering support played a significant role in his journey. "It's a massive milestone, and I'm humbled to have achieved it," he stated in a recent interview. The 30-year-old New Zealander has been instrumental in Black Caps' success, leading the team to several victories and helping the nation achieve its cricketing aspirations. Bracewell's achievements will undoubtedly be celebrated by cricket fans across the globe, as he continues to inspire and motivate his teammates. His remarkable journey is a testament to the power of perseverance and dedication.
