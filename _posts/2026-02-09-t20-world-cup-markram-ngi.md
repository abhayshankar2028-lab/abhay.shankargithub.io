In a thrilling match at the T20 World Cup, South Africa continued its impressive form with a resounding 55-run victory over their arch-rivals Canada. In the shortest format, South Africa's openers, Rassie van Assag and Aiden Markram, set the tone with a 51-run opening stand. Markram, who had a stellar tournament so far, continued his impressive form with 11 runs from 22 deliveries. The Canadians, led by Nicholas Pooran, struggled to contain the South African attack, particularly the leg-spinner Anrich Nortje, who picked up crucial wickets. South Africa's all-rounder, Aiden Markram, also contributed with a crucial 29 runs. The win moves South Africa to the top of the T20 World Cup standings, while Canada falls to third place. This victory has set the stage for a thrilling semifinal match against Australia, which is slated for later today.
