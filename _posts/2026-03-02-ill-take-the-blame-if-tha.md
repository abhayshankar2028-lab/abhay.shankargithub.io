Shai Hope's frustration was palpable as he stood by his captain's decision to retire from T20 cricket ahead of the 2023 T20 World Cup. "I'll take the blame if that's what you want me to say," Hope said in an exclusive interview. The 30-year-old West Indian opener was visibly upset over the decision, which left many questioning his future in the team. "I feel like we're not giving it enough time to breathe," Hope expressed his concerns. The decision has sparked a heated debate within the team, with some teammates questioning the timing and others supporting the move. Hope's comments have highlighted the tension and unrest within the squad as they bid farewell to the T20 era and embark on a new phase.
