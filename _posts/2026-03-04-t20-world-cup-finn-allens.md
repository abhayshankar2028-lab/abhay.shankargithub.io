In a thrilling T20 World Cup, New Zealand has made history by reaching the final for the first time in their history. The Black Caps were humbled by a record-breaking display from Finn Allen, who scored an astonishing 57 off just 33 deliveries to seal a dominant victory over South Africa. Allen's incredible innings, coupled with a winning partnership from Kane Williamson, saw New Zealand post a total of 202/6 in 18.2 overs. The Kiwis' bowling attack, led by Adam Zampa, restricted South Africa to just 140/9. New Zealand's victory marks their first T20 World Cup final appearance, and they will face arch-rivals England in the championship match. This historic triumph cements Finn Allen's status as one of the world's leading T20 batsmen, and he is likely to be in the running for the ICC T20I Team of the Tournament.
