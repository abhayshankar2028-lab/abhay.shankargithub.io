Sanju Samson, the Indian wicket-keeper batsman, has been in phenomenal form throughout the tournament. With 157 runs and 3 wickets at an economy rate of 8.93, Samson has proven himself to be one of the top performers in the competition.

While it's a historic moment for Indian cricket, it's worth considering whether Samson can replicate his form in the long run. Can he continue to excel at this level and join the club of Virat Kohli and Jasprit Bumrah, the two other top players from the Indian team?

It will be interesting to see how India performs in the next few years and whether Samson can cement his place in the cricketing fraternity.
