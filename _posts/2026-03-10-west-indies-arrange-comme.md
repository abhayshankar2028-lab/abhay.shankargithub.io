**West Indies Players Fined for Delayed Departure**

In a shocking turn of events, the West Indies cricket team has been left stranded in India for the past nine days after their ICC charter flight was delayed. The team, comprising players like Jason Holder and Darren Sammy, were supposed to arrive in Delhi yesterday, but their schedule has been disrupted.

The delay has resulted in a significant financial burden on the players, who were also left to spend their free time in the city. The West Indies Cricket Board (WICB) has expressed disappointment and frustration over the situation. "We are extremely disappointed that the charter flight has been delayed," said a WICB spokesperson. "The players are now left to spend their time in India, which is not ideal for their personal and professional well-being."
