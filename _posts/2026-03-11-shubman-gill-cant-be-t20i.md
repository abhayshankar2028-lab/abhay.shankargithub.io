![‘Shubman Gill can’t be T20I captain anymore’: Former opener rules out all-format captain for India after T20 World Cup win](https://images.indianexpress.com/2026/03/gill-ap-1_20260311084003.jpg)

In a recent interview, former India opener Shubman Gill has expressed his utmost disappointment and concern over the possibility of him being considered as the all-format captain for the Indian cricket team. Gill, who led the team to their impressive victory in the T20 World Cup, stated that he can no longer consider himself as the ideal candidate. He emphasized that being an all-format captain is a significant role and requires a level of experience and adaptability that he cannot match. "I'm not saying I'm not qualified, but I'm just not the right person for it," Gill said. As the country will soon be gearing up for the ODI and Test series, Gill's sentiments may be felt by fans and cricket enthusiasts alike. His candid response has sparked debate among Indians and cricket experts, leaving many questioning whether a new captain should be chosen for the team.
