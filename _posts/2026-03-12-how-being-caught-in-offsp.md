![How being caught in off-spin trap led to India bringing back Sanju Samson: ‘We decided to go with two wicketkeepers’](https://images.indianexpress.com/2026/03/samson-gambhir-pti.jpg)

In a significant move, India has made Sanju Samson their new wicketkeeper, replacing MS Dhoni in the squad. The decision to bring back Samson was largely influenced by a dramatic turn of events at the hands of off-spinner Kuldeep Yadav, who got Samson out in a crucial match. With Dhoni's eventual departure, Samson stepped into the role and impressed with his performance. The onus was to replace Dhoni, and Samson's consistency with the bat and glove proved to be the deciding factor. India's decision to go with two wicketkeepers may seem unconventional, but it's a bold move that could pay off in the long run. The team is confident that Samson's experience and skills will be a game-changer in their upcoming matches.
