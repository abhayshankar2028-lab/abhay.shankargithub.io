Rahul Dravid's 2001 Test century at Eden Gardens will forever be etched in cricketing history. In a remarkable innings, the India captain achieved a remarkable 138 not out, as his team embarked on a thrilling chase against Australia. What makes this milestone even more special is that it came on a day when the Indian team was reeling, having lost their only wicket in the first six overs. Despite facing unimaginable pressure, Dravid played an innings for the ages, showcasing his incredible batting prowess. The Eden Gardens crowd, known for their electric atmosphere, was abuzz with excitement as Dravid dominated the proceedings, ultimately guiding India to a thrilling victory. This iconic moment will forever be remembered as a testament to Dravid's remarkable dedication and skill on the cricket pitch.
