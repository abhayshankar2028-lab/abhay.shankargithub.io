In a recent move, the BCCI's Vice-President Rajeev Shukla expressed his reservations about the Sunrisers Hyderabad's (SRH) signing of Pakistan's Abrar Ahmed. Shukla stated that the move could cause trouble for India's domestic cricket, citing concerns about the opposition SRH team faces in the league. He also voiced his concerns about the potential disruption to India's overseas cricket schedule. Shukla emphasized that interference in the SRH signings is not something that the BCCI can tolerate and urged the board to take a more measured approach. He stated that the decision should be based on the team's performance and not influenced by external factors. The BCCI's stance on the matter highlights the importance of maintaining a level playing field for all teams in the cricket world.
