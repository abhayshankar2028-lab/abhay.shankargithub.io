**A Regrettable Decision: Former Aussie Coach's Take on Enforcing Follow-On**

In a candid interview, former Australian cricket coach George Bailey revealed that one of his most regrettable decisions was enforcing follow-on (FL) against India in the 2001 Kolkata Test. This often-anticipated counter-innings proved disastrous, ultimately leading to Australia's 72-run defeat. Bailey stated, "It was one of my poorest decisions as a coach... We knew Indians were capable of rebuilding their innings, and following on was a way to do that."

The decision sparked widespread criticism, and Bailey acknowledged that the Australians "should have dictated the terms of the game" rather than introducing FL. This relatively minor blunder in an otherwise impressive Ashes series ultimately cost Bailey his job. The incident serves as a reminder that even the best coaches can make mistakes, and it's essential to analyze the data and learn from these experiences to improve team performance.
