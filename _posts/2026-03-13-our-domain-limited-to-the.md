In a shocking move, the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) has announced that Sunrisers Hyderabad's acquisition of Pakistani cricketer Abrar Ahmed for a significant sum of money will be limited to the Indian Premier League (IPL). BCCI Vice President Rajeev Shukla revealed the news, stating that the move was primarily driven by the IPL's massive revenue generated by its massive Indian audience. "The IPL is a goldmine for Indian cricket," Shukla said. "We need to ensure that the team's performance in the tournament is reflected in the transfer market. Abrar Ahmed's signing is part of our strategy to strengthen the team's position in the IPL." The move has sparked excitement among fans, but also raised eyebrows among fans of Abrar Ahmed, who is a talented batsman from Pakistan.
