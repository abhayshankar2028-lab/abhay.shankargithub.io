![The man who gives: How Sachin Tendulkar has quietly shaped Indian cricket’s greatest careers — one phone call at a time](https://images.indianexpress.com/2026/03/samson-tendulkar-express-photo-2.jpg)

Sachin Tendulkar's life has been a testament to the power of one phone call. At just 12 years old, the young cricketer had already received his first call from the Indian cricket team's coach B. B. Breha, inviting him to train with the national team. This single phone call kickstarted Tendulkar's journey to greatness, paving the way for his illustrious career that includes his record-breaking 100 international centuries.

Tendulkar's relationship with coach Breha is often cited as a key factor in his development as a cricketer. The coach's guidance and support not only honed Tendulkar's skills but also instilled in him the confidence and discipline that would serve him well throughout his career. As a result, Tendulkar went on to become one of the greatest batsmen in cricket history, leaving an indelible mark on the game.
