![Indian team prioritises collective success over personal milestones: Sanju Samson](https://images.indianexpress.com/2026/03/SANJU-SAMSON-EXPRESS-PHOTO.jpg)

In an exclusive interview, Sanju Samson, India's wicketkeeper batsman, emphasized the team's collective approach to success. "We're not just about individual milestones; we're about winning games," Samson said. The 30-year-old batsman highlighted the importance of supporting teammates and working towards a common goal. "We've got a great team dynamic, and we all know that our success is linked to our individual performance," he added. Samson's comments echoed his team's strategy, where they focus on win-loss records rather than personal achievements. "We're not trying to win individual titles; we want to win as a team," he stated. This approach aligns with the Indian cricket setup, where success is measured by team performance rather than individual records. Samson's words underscore the team's collective mindset, driving them to work towards a shared goal.
