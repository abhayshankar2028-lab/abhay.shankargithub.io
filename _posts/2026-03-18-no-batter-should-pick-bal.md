![‘No batter should pick ball without consent’: MCC explains Salman Agha run-out after spirit of cricket debate](https://images.indianexpress.com/2026/03/PAK-BAN-AGHA-DISMISSAL.jpg)

In a recent incident that has sparked a heated debate about the spirit of cricket, the Match Committee of the Marylebone Cricket Club (MCC) has issued a stern warning to Salman Agha. Agha was involved in a run-out that resulted in a bat being knocked off his arm, leaving him limping off the field. The MCC has now stated that a batter should never pick up the ball without the other batsman's consent.

The statement emphasizes the importance of a player's reflexes and reaction time, highlighting that a picked-up ball can be a result of a batsman's own error. The MCC's stance on the matter is a reflection of the club's commitment to maintaining the integrity and fair play of the game. This warning serves as a reminder to all players of the importance of respecting their fellow batsmen and adhering to the rules of the game.
