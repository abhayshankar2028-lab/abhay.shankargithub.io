In a recent interview, Jos Buttler opened up about England's T20 World Cup performance, hinting at a future without the current skipper. Buttler stated that "what will be, will be" for England's cricket future, emphasizing that the team will move forward without him as the primary wicket-keeper.

The 32-year-old England batsman took a swipe at his own performance, stating that he was "not happy" with his T20 form. However, he also expressed confidence in the team's core, stating that "things will get better" and that the England selectors will find a suitable replacement for him in the future.

With England's T20 World Cup campaign ending in a disappointing fourth-place finish, it appears that Buttler's time as the top wicket-keeper might be coming to an end. His words suggest that he is willing to move on and let the selectors find the right fit for the role.
