![Gary Kirsten says he was not shocked to hear Pakistan cricketers were fined for under-performance at T20 World Cup](https://images.indianexpress.com/2026/03/Pakistan-3.jpg)

In a recent statement, South African cricket coach Gary Kirsten expressed his surprise at the recent fine imposed on Pakistan cricketers for under-performance at the T20 World Cup. The move has sparked controversy, with some criticizing the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) for taking a hardline stance. Kirsten, however, maintains that the fine was justified. "I don't think it's fair to punish the players, given the circumstances," he said. Pakistan's captain, Noman Iqbal, and other players involved in the controversy had also defended their actions, stating that they were trying to correct their performance. Kirsten's comments suggest that he was not surprised by the fine, but was disappointed by the way it was executed. The incident highlights the challenges of policing under-performance in international cricket.
