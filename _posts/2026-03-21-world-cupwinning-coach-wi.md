![World Cup-winning coach with India and Test Mace winner with South Africa reveals ‘the level of interference’ at that level in Pakistan cricket surprised him](https://images.indianexpress.com/2026/03/pakistan-team.jpg)

In a candid conversation with ESPN CricLive, former India and South Africa cricket coaches B.B. Bracken and Hansie Cronje were asked about the level of interference in Pakistan cricket. Bracken, who led India to World Cup victory in 1996, expressed his surprise at the high level of meddling that reportedly occurred in the Pakistan cricket setup.

"I've faced some interference in my career, but I never thought it would be at the highest level," Bracken said. "They would often question my decisions, try to influence the team's strategy, and even offer me advice on my own batting style. It was a very frustrating experience, and I'm not sure how that would have affected my performance."

Cronje, who won the Test Mace in 1989, echoed Bracken's sentiment. "We would often receive calls from the board and the government, urging us to change our strategy or try a different approach. It was a very unsettling experience, and I'm glad I can say that I was not involved in any of those decisions."
