In a surprising move, Cricket Australia has announced that it will continue to allow local curators to prepare home Test surfaces despite criticism that the process is "bad for business." The decision comes after a heated debate between the governing body and local cricket boards, who argued that the process was lengthy and costly. However, Cricket Australia emphasizes that the preparation process is crucial to the success of Test matches. Local curators, such as the Cricket Australia Curators, will continue to prepare home surfaces, including the iconic MCG and SCG stadiums. The decision ensures that local cricket boards have the opportunity to develop a deeper understanding of the game and prepare their own surfaces. This move is expected to benefit the local cricket community and promote grassroots cricket. The decision has been welcomed by local cricket boards.
