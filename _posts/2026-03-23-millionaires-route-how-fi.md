**Millionaire's Route: How Five Youngsters Battled the Odds to Arrive at the IPL**

It's not every day that a group of five young cricketers from different states and backgrounds makes their way to the Indian Premier League (IPL) as millionaire cricketers. Meet the quintet: Rohit Sharma, Mayank Agarwal, Hardik Pandya, Umesh Yadav, and Bhuvneshwar Kumar.

Each of them had to overcome incredible odds to reach the IPL. Rohit, the Mumbai Indians captain, had to leave behind a coaching career to pursue a cricketing career. Mayank, the Delhi Capitals star, had to start from the bottom, playing for several teams before cracking the IPL draft. Hardik, the Delhi Capitals and Lucknow Super Giants vice-captain, had to work multiple jobs to save up for his cricketing career. Umesh, the Rajasthan Royals and Royal Challengers Bangalore pacer, was a former Mumbai Ranaghat XI player. And Bhuvneshwar, the Delhi Capitals and Royal Challengers Bangalore fast bowler, was a Chandigarh-based player who worked as a security guard.

Their determination and perseverance paid off, and they are now million-dollar cricketers, making millions from the IPL.
