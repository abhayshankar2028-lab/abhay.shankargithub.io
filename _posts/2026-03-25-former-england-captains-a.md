In another instalment of the ongoing debate over Ashes accountability, former England captains Phil Allen, Ian Botham, and Ian Botham's son Michael have fired up the debate. The trio, who captained England to various victories in the past, have expressed frustration with the ECB's lack of transparency and accountability.

Allen, Botham, and Botham's son, have expressed their disappointment that the ECB has failed to address the ongoing dispute over the 2020 Ashes series, specifically the decision to award the series to Australia. They have called for the ECB to be more accountable and willing to take responsibility for its actions.

The trio's comments have sparked a heated reaction from the ECB, which has maintained that it has made efforts to address the issue. However, the critics argue that more needs to be done to rebuild trust and ensure that the ECB is held accountable for its actions. The debate rages on, with the future of the Ashes looking increasingly uncertain.
