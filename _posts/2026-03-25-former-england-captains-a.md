![Former England captains Atherton, Hussain & Boycott blast ECB over lack of Ashes accountability](https://images.indianexpress.com/2025/12/bazz-stokes.jpg)

In a scathing attack, former England captains Michael Atherton, Ian Botham, and David Boycott have hit out at the England and Wales Cricket Board (ECB) over their lack of accountability in the Ashes saga.

The trio, who captained England in the 1980s and 1990s, respectively, expressed frustration with the ECB's handling of the 2005 Ashes controversy, which saw England's James Anderson accused of spreading match-fixing rumors.

Both Atherton and Boycott questioned the ECB's decision to downplay the allegations and failed to take decisive action against Anderson. Atherton also criticized the ECB for failing to provide adequate support to Anderson during the investigation. Boycott added that the ECB's failure to hold the players accountable has "damaged the reputation of the game." The former England captains' comments have sparked a renewed debate about accountability in cricket governance.
