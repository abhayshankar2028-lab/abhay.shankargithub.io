![‘They called me panauti’: Dia Mirza recalls being tagged ‘bad luck’, says ‘I told Rajkumar Hirani I have no work’](https://images.indianexpress.com/2026/03/dia-mirza_91291f.jpg)

In a candid conversation with The Indian Express, actress Dia Mirza shared a laugh-filled tale of being called "panauti" - a term of derogatory affection that's often used to express bad luck. According to her, she was often teased by her friends and family, who would say things like "you're a bad luck charm" or "may your career pan out badly." Mirza recounts an instance where she tried to brush it off, saying, "I told my friend Rajkumar Hirani, 'I have no work, so I must be the bad luck charm'." Mirza chuckles, saying it was a light-hearted joke, but the teasing still lingered. She attributes the negativity to the pressures of her film industry career. "I think it's essential to be aware of the words we use and how they affect others," she advises. Mirza's lighthearted take on the experience serves as a reminder to laugh off the jabs and focus on our own journeys.
