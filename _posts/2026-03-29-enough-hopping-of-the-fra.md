Hardik Pandya's impressive match-winning performance for Mumbai Indians (MI) against Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR) has sparked a heated debate regarding the franchise's player retention. Pandya, who took 3 crucial wickets and scored 27 runs in the 18.3 overs, has expressed his desire to stay with the team for the rest of his career. In a recent statement, Pandya mentioned that he feels "enough" hopping has been done and that staying with MI for the full season is a "no-brainer." This sentiment is echoed by MI's coach, Stephen Fry, who has also stated that Pandya is committed to the team. As a result, it appears that Shardul Thakur will have to play out the remainder of the season with MI. This move is expected to benefit both Pandya and the team, as it will ensure his dedication and involvement in the team's success.
