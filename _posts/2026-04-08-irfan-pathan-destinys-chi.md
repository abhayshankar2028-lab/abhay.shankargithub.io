Irfan Pathan, the 'Destiny's child', is a name that echoes with India's cricketing legacy. With 100 Test caps and 137 ODI caps under his belt, Irfan has etched his mark on the game. His impressive career has seen him excel in various formats, showcasing his versatility and skillset. The 35-year-old all-rounder has been an integral part of India's success, contributing to their Ashes triumphs and World Cup victories. His leadership qualities and ability to adapt to different conditions have made him a valuable asset to the team. It's time for Vaibhav Sooryavanshi to don the India cap, acknowledging his own journey and potential. Irfan's legacy serves as a benchmark, and it's crucial that Vaibhav strives to match his success. Giving him an India cap now would be a fitting tribute to his dedication and hard work.
