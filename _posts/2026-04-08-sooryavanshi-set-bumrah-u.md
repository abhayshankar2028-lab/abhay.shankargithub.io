In a stunning display of batting prowess, Rohit Sharma's 165 not out showed glimpses of greatness, but it was the unlikeliest of heroes, Jasprit Bumrah, who set the record straight. The Indian pacer's exploits down the order sent shockwaves through the cricketing world. Bumrah, who has been in a slump, took 4/48 in 22 balls, with a whopping 6 fours and 3 sixes, showcasing his pinpoint accuracy. Anil Kumble, India's cricketing legend, dissected Bumrah's performance, pointing out several crucial aspects that contributed to his impressive haul. "Sooryavanshi set Bumrah up, in terms of the conditions and the opposition," Anil said. "Bumrah played some brilliant shots, but his length and pace were too much for the batsmen." Kumble's analysis highlights Bumrah's growth and potential as one of the world's best bowlers.
