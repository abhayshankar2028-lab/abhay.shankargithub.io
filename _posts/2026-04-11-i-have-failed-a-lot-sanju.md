In an exclusive interview, former Chennai Super Kings (CSK) batter Sanju Samson spoke candidly about his struggles, including his first century against the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB). The 29-year-old Indian opener reflected on the numerous failures he faced, stating, "I have failed a lot." However, he took a moment to express gratitude by paying homage to Sir Donald Bradman, often referred to as the 'Greatest Cricketer of All Time.' Samson quipped, "I salute Sir Don, the great one." He humorously added, "But, I hope he doesn't mind I'm not as good as him." Samson's lighthearted approach to failure resonated with fans, and he acknowledged that even the greatest cricketers have experienced setbacks. His humility and willingness to learn from his mistakes made his statement all the more endearing.
