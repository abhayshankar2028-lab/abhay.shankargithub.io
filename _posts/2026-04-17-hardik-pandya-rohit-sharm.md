As the IPL season continues, Hardik Pandya and Rohit Sharma have been at the center of attention for their impressive performances. However, a Cricket Focus observer notes that the duo is not the sole reason for the Mumbai Indians' struggles. "Hardik and Rohit are just two of the many talented players on the team," says Mahela Jayawardene, a former Sri Lankan captain. "The real issue lies with the ownership and management of the team. They need to make changes to get the team back on track."

Jayawardene, who played for the Mumbai Indians from 2007 to 2009, emphasizes the importance of a strong backroom staff, better training facilities, and a clear strategy to drive results. The veteran cricketer also criticizes the team's lack of depth, suggesting that the Indians need to build a stronger squad to compete at the top level.
