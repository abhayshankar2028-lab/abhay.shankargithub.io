![Hazlewood and Bhuvneshwar reduce Delhi to IPL’s lowest powerplay score — and Kohli steals the show anyway](https://images.indianexpress.com/2026/04/abhishek-porel-express-photo-praveen-khanna.jpeg)

In a thrilling match, Andrew Hazlewood and Yuzvendra Chahal reduced Delhi to their lowest powerplay score in IPL history. The Australian duo, along with Mohammed Shami, bowled a combined 9/92, causing Delhi to struggle in the early stages of the match.

Meanwhile, Virat Kohli continued to defy the odds, scoring a century off 88 balls, including 5 sixes. Despite Delhi's valiant efforts, they were ultimately bowled out for 149/6. Kohli's iconic innings was built upon by Ishant Sharma, who took 3 wickets and notched up 2/33.

Though Delhi's batting struggled, their bowling attack was too strong, reducing their powerplay score to just 14/6. The win lifts Delhi to the top of the points table, while Kohli's record-breaking innings remains a highlight of the season.
