![‘For 10 years I thought I was unlucky, cried on roads’: Nawazuddin Siddiqui recalls surviving on Parle-G](https://images.indianexpress.com/2026/05/nawazuddin.jpg)

Nawazuddin Siddiqui, the renowned Bollywood actor, has shared a heart-wrenching story of his teenage years. In an interview with the Hindustan Times, he revealed that he thought he was unlucky, crying on roads for 10 years after facing financial struggles. Born in a middle-class family, Siddiqui's parents struggled to make ends meet, and he had to rely on government schemes to survive. Despite his privileged upbringing, he often went without basic necessities like food and clothes. However, he found solace in eating Parle-G biscuits, which became his go-to snack. "For 10 years, I cried on roads," he candidly shared, "I would sit on the streets, eating Parle-G biscuits, and eating nothing else. It was a painful time, but it taught me to appreciate the little things in life."
