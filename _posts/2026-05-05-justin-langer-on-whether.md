Justin Langer, the renowned Australian cricket coach, has sparked controversy with his recent comments on high-scoring chases in international cricket. In an interview, Langer expressed his disappointment and frustration with the trend of high-profile batting teams dominating the game. He stated that batsmen are now more comfortable scoring runs at will, without worrying about defending a target. Langer believes that this approach leads to a lack of defensive cricket and a decrease in the psychological aspect of the game.

While some might argue that high-scoring chases can bring excitement and entertainment to the game, Langer's sentiments carry weight. He's right; the batsmen, often with the support of large crowds and the pressure of being in a high-stakes situation, have become complacent and risk-averse. Langer's comments raise questions about the value of these thrilling matches and the impact they have on the sport's overall quality.
