**The Relentless Legacy of Mughal-e-Azam: A Film that Never Recovered**

Mughal-e-Azam, released in 1960, was a cinematic gamble that catapulted Aamir Khan to stardom, but ultimately proved a costly misstep. The film's budget was pegged at Rs 145 crore, a staggering sum at the time. The production was marred by delays, technical issues, and a massive overhaul of the story, which led to a significant rewrite of the script and reshoots. As a result, the film underwent 12 changes, and the final product was significantly different from the original vision. The film's success was short-lived, and Aamir Khan's subsequent films struggled to match its level of excellence. The financial repercussions of this gamble still linger, a cautionary tale about the perils of taking risks in the film industry.
