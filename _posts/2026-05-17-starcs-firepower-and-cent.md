In a thrilling victory for the Delhi Capitals, Mitchell Starc's dominating bowling display continued to play a pivotal role in keeping the team's hopes alive. Starc's century opening stand with Mandeep Singh proved to be the turning point in the match, as they took the Capitals to 221/5 at lunch. North London, who were struggling with 2/5 in the first 40 overs, slowly began to rebuild their innings.

However, it was Starc who was instrumental in turning the tide. He picked up 3 wickets in the 7th over, and then 2 more in the 11th over, showcasing his remarkable speed and accuracy. The Capitals' captain, Rishabh Pant, also played a crucial role in the innings, scoring 87 runs at a strike rate of 122. Starc's performances have been key to the team's revival, and they will be hoping to build on this momentum as they face their next opponents.
