**Revisiting the Blush-worthy Love Story: Rewatching Kartik Aaryan's Pyaar Ka Punchnama**

As I rewound the nostalgia-filled moments of Pyaar Ka Punchnama, I couldn't help but cringe at the misguided portrayal of love and relationships. The film, released in 2011, was a prime example of the misogynistic tropes that Bollywood perpetuated during its heyday. Kartik Aaryan's character, Ayaan, was a bully who mercilessly belittled the women around him, often using derogatory language and suggestive innuendos.

While the film's plot was engaging, its problematic elements overshadowed the romance and chemistry between the leads. I'm relieved that the film has been reevaluated in recent years, with many praising its action-packed and emotional moments. However, I still find myself disappointed by the film's handling of sensitive topics. Nevertheless, Pyaar Ka Punchnama remains a guilty pleasure that I revisit every now and then, a reminder of the era when love was equated with violence and aggression.
