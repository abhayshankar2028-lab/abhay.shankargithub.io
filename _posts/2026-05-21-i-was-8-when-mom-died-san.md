"I was 8 when my mother, Naneet Dutt, took her last breath. It's a painful memory that still lingers. As a child of a 'silver spoon' family, I grew up with a life of luxury and privilege. But, one thing that I never knew was the harsh reality of our childhood. My parents made me sleep in a room that was identical to the one I'm sleeping in today, but it's a far cry from the opulent life we led. Our household was a monotonous routine of school, television, and dinner. Life was simple, yet suffocating. I was forced to be a dutiful daughter, never daring to express my feelings or desires. It's a dark reality that I'm glad to have escaped, but I wish I could have known a childhood free of such hardships."
