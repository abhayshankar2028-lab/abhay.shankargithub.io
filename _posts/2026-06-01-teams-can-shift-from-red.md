**Teams Can Switch to Pink Balls in Test Matches to Deal with Bad Light**

In the ongoing trend of adapting to changing weather conditions in Test cricket, teams have started experimenting with pink balls instead of the traditional red balls. This innovative move aims to mitigate the effects of bad light and harsh weather conditions during the game. Pink balls have been used in several international matches, including those in India and New Zealand, and have shown promising results.

The switch to pink balls is said to reduce glare on the ball, making it easier for bowlers to deliver the ball, and also reduce the risk of overs being reduced due to the ball's condition. While the results are still to be seen, the introduction of pink balls in Test cricket has set a new benchmark for teams to adapt to changing weather conditions.
